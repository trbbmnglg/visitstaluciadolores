Sacred Heritage

The Jerusalem of the Philippines

Long before it became a destination for trekkers, the slopes of Mt. Banahaw were a sanctuary for revolutionaries and spiritual seekers alike. Known locally as the "Banal na Lugar", Dolores is considered by many as the spiritual center of the region.

Sta. Lucia is the heart of this mysticism. It serves as the traditional gateway for the "Pamumuwesto"—a pilgrimage undertaken specifically during Holy Week. Pilgrims, or mamumuwesto, follow a strict route of sacred stations (puestos), including caves, rocks, and springs, praying at each site to wash away sins and recharge their spiritual energy.

The journey typically begins at the Sta. Lucia Falls. Here, believers perform a ritual bath under the icy waters, symbolizing purification before they are allowed to ascend the higher, more sacred levels of the mountain towards "Durungawan".

Respect the Sacred Mountain

Mt. Banahaw is not just a hiking destination but a holy sanctuary for pilgrims. We ask all visitors to honor its sanctity and help preserve its pristine nature.

Leave No Trace

2. Drop off at San Pablo City (7-Eleven/Franklin Baker junction).

Best Time to Visit

Dry Season (Jan - May)

Perfect for trekking and camping. Trails are less slippery, and the river water is crystal clear. This is the ideal time for general tourists.

Holy Week (March/April)

The peak season for pilgrims. Expect large crowds of devotees (mamumuwesto). A unique cultural experience if you don't mind the hustle.

Festivals

Hambujan Festival (April): Celebrates local unity and religious thanksgiving.
Niyogyugan (August): Provincial-wide coconut festival.

Good to Know

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there souvenir shops?
Yes. There are lot of souvenir shops just before going to the Sta. Lucia falls.
Do I need a tour guide to hike?
Yes. For major treks, hiring a local guide is mandatory for your safety and to respect the sacred grounds. For simple visits to the falls or river, guides are optional but recommended.
Is there mobile signal in the area?
Signal is available in the barangay proper and some open areas, but it becomes weak or non-existent as you go higher up the mountain or deep into the valleys. SMART is currently the best and most reliable network in Sta. Lucia. We suggest informing your family beforehand.
Are there ATMs in Sta. Lucia?
No. The nearest ATMs are located in the Dolores town proper, which is a tricycle ride away. Please withdraw sufficient cash before heading to Sta. Lucia.
Is GCash available?
Yes. Local stores offer GCash Cash In/Cash Out services. Many establishments also accept GCash payments.
Is there a talipapa nearby?
Yes, there is a local talipapa in the barangay where you can buy fresh vegetables and fruits. For meats, they mostly sell chicken and pork. Fish is also available.
What should I bring for a day trip?
We recommend bringing:
  • Extra clothes (you will likely get wet)
  • Trekking sandals or sturdy shoes
  • Water bottle (reusable)
  • Small trash bag (Clean As You Go policy)
  • Cash (small bills for entrance fees/donations)
Is there parking for private vehicles?
Yes, there is an open parking area available for visitors. The parking fee is a fixed rate of ₱50.
Is there a vulcanizing shop in Sta. Lucia?
In case of flat tires or repairs, the nearest is in the town proper.
Is there a fuel station in Sta. Lucia?
The nearest is in the town proper at NTN Dolores Gas Station.
